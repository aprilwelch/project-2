About

I have been very fortunate in life. My work is fun and my fun is meaningful. All aspects of my life tend to ebb and flow together in harmony. Currently I am the Director of Business and Educational planning for the Idea Shop at the Illinois Institute of Technology. I am committed to providing an open and flexible learning environment, in which technology seamlessly supports the creative process. The Idea Shop is a configurable collaborative learning space designed to facilitate teamwork and innovation. It is equipped with a 3D rapid prototyping lab, digital media center, iPad library and wall-to-wall IdeaPaint. The Idea Shop is the ideal space for bringing thoughts to reality through idea generation, design thinking, and iterative processing.

Additionally, it is my priviledge to serve as the Program Director for the Exelon Summer Institute at IIT. The Exelon Summer Institute is designed to create a diverse and supportive learning community for first-year college students. This endowed program provides specific strategies for college success, mentorship, and one-on-one tutoring supporting students throughout the academic year. For the past two years I have also organized the Academy for Future Leaders in Science and Technology. This program was made possible by a grant from Toyota.

A brief history... I recieved my Bachelor of Science in Business Administration with a concentration in marketing and communication from Boston University’s School of Management. I was fortunate to land a job in my field right out of college. I worked as part of the worldwide marketing team at Arthur Andersen‘s headquarters in Chicago. Later, I was part of the Educational Technology team at Andersen Consulting (now Accenture). While working full-time I earned my Bachelor of Art in Fine Arts (Photography and Graphic Design) from Loyola University, Chicago. My career then led me to DePaul University where I designed and delivered the Accelerated e-business training program for at risk students and helped with the training component of a PeopleSoft implementation. While at DePaul I earned my Master of Art in Applied Professional Studies with a focus on Instructional Design. My desire to continue writing new training programs for youth led me to the Illinois Institute of Technology. While here I earned my Master of Science in Technical Communication and Information Design. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at IIT pursuing a culminating degree in Technical Communications.
