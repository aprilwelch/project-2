Portfolio - Kung Fu

Like many Chicagoans my first introduction to Kung Fu was WGN’s Samurai Sunday, there were rarely any Samurai. There were, however, endless hours of amazing Kung Fu artists. I really liked the old movies where they had one static camera in the distance so you could see full movements and complete motions. No quick cuts, no heavy editing, no special effects just real guys showing off years of practice. Then I saw a Bruce Lee movie as a girl and I was hooked. My mom was in the Marine Corps reserve stationed at the 2/24 headquarters and she talked Sargent Major Wilburn into letting me join his Karate for Women class even though I was 10 years old. This was perfect timing as I still had the fearlessness of youth and I found that I was able to do everything the women did. My attention shifted to field hockey during my high school years but when I went to college I choose Kung Fu.

Master Bow Sim Mark taught at Boston University, and I would go to her school in Chinatown on the weekends. http://www.taichiarts.com/ Sifu is an amazing person. She is the perfect combination of grace, beauty and power. I am forever grateful for the time I was able to study with her. During those years, I also took Long Fist from Sifu Rick Wong. http://www.rickwongsmartialarts.com/SifuRickWong.html. He was very dedicated and he spent extra time beyond our sessions helping those of us in his class who stayed late every session. After college I did some traveling before coming back home to Chicago.

I tried different schools here in Chicago but nothing felt right, in part because Master Mark was so incredible. I decided to learn another style all together. I was working at DePaul when I found the right fit, on that instantly felt right. First I met Sifu Ed de la Cruz and then Sifu Ken Weingart and then Sifu Mountain Ed. They were teaching Wing Chun in our sports center at work after the classes ended I wanted more. I’ve never looked for another school, I found my home with Windy City. http://windycitywingchun.com/ The foundational material the three Sifu's were sharing came from the incredible Sifu Augustine Fong. Over the years I have had the great pleasure of traveling to Tucson, AZ to learn from him as well. He is a visionary and he has openly shared his talents, experience and skill. He only asks that students use his methods with a good heart and be sincere in all they do. http://www.fongswingchun.com/

Sifu Augustine Fong spent year learning from Master Ho Kam Ming who spent years learning the complete Wing Chun System from Grand Master Yip Man. http://www.tempewingchun.com/teaching.html

If you are interested in Wing Chun I would highly recommend you consider taking a trip to Tucson, AZ. Sifu Fong openly invites people from all disciplines to come study with him.

The trips to Tucson are always rich and rewarding experiences. Like everything in life you get out of it what you put into it. Sifu Fong answers question in exactly the way you would expect a Kung Fu master to.

Windy City Wing Chun has been a wonderful part of my life. Like-minded people tend to gravitate to each other. Over the years of being together we find that Kung Fu is not the only thing we have in common.

Sifu Ed and Sifu Ken have been like brothers to me over the years. I am very grateful to have rendezvoused with them. I am looking forward to many more years together. Check out www.windycitywingchun.com

My time with Master Mark was my favorite part of my college years. I spent as much time with her as I possibly could. This video however does not reflect that. I was so honored that she chose me to be at her side with her daughter for this special. I was also super nervous when the lights came on and the cameras started rolling. I was stumbling on moves I had done flawlessly in class hundreds of times! I felt like I totally let her down in the first video. She comforted me and I got better in the later sessions.

I learned a fan form for a performance once but I had never seen the form executed in the way this team did at the Lenovo pavilion outside the Bird’s Nest during the Olympics in Beijing. They were all young men in the prime of their lives performing with an intensity and precision that was just awesome to watch.

This is another team doing one of the forms they taught us in Boston. The music they are using was very popular for competitions at the time and the team does a great job staying in sync with each other.

When I was in China I took a train and a bus and a buggy to get to the original Shaolin temple. Kung Fu Panda came to mind when I saw all the stairs. There is actually a real rope bridge too! I took this video outside the temple, I was not allowed behind the wall. My “but they are so cute” reason for being there was not enough to grant me passage.

According to http://www.travelchinaguide.com "The Shaolin Temple, located on the Shaoshi Mountain in the west of Dengfeng County, Henan Province, is the 'ancestor court' of Chinese Zen Buddhism. During the Southern and Northern Dynasties (386-589), the Indian monk Bodhidharma, a profound exponent of Zen Buddhism came to China. Emperor Xiaowendi of Northern Wei gave him a very courteous reception. Later, in 496, the emperor decreed that a Buddhist temple be built on the Shaoshi Mountain and food be supplied to the monks. It is today's Shaolin Temple."
