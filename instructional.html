Portfolio - Instructional Design

Throughout my life I have often had the priviledge to write, design and deliver training programs. Below is a selection of programs that I am very proud of. As with everything worthwhile there are many people that contribute to the success of these programs.

2013 Exelon Summer Institute

Now in its 6th year, the Exelon Summer Institute is a recognized model for supporting students transitioning to college life. The program is built around the concept of creating a community of learners in the areas of math, physics, chemistry, writing and computer skills. Student teaching teams interact with new students while faculty members conduct specialized lectures and participate in field trips. It is a hands-on program that includes a rocket project, robot wars, and bridge and catapult challenges. In addition, there are mini-projects dispersed throughout the day that give our students an opportunity to flex their intellect with ingenuity and creativity.

The transition to university life and the academic rigors of the first year of college can be challenging. The Exelon Summer Institute is an intensive 4-week program designed to give new students an opportunity to meet current students, make friends and experience college life at IIT. Incoming first year students also benefit from meeting faculty and doing college-level class work in preparation for the fall semester. Additionally during the program new students learn about the city and all the resources available on campus.

Academy for Future Leaders in Science and Technology

The Academy for Future Leaders in Science and Technology is a 6-week science and technology education and internship program geared toward women and minorities from socio-economically disadvantaged backgrounds. The mission of the academy is to achieve greater workforce diversity in the professional fields especially those of environmental science and management by providing opportunities aimed at enriching the academic, social and professional competencies of students who are typically under-represented in higher education science and technology fields.

The goals of the Academy are to create a framework of success that builds a foundation for the student's lifelong achievement by: 1) Increasing access to Environmental Science and Management education; 2) Providing the mentoring support necessary to motivate students to complete their high school education and continue on with post-secondary education, especially in the areas of science, technology and Environmental Science and Management; and 3) Developing awareness of "real world" opportunities in science, technology, and Environmental Science and Management through exposure to existing and emerging careers in this field.

Freelance Coder Camp

The Freelance Coder Course is a unique opportunity to creatively learn the business of becoming a successful freelance computer coder and gain entrepreneurial skills that could lead to earning supplemental income. This project-based six-week course also offers continued learning opportunities through internships and workshops. Held on the IIT Main Campus, the course provides access to industry experts and the latest technology. The course also includes field trips, guest speakers and networking events.

Motorola Math Camp

The Summer Math Camp at IIT supported by the Motorola Foundation is an intensive six-week program designed to help incoming first-year students, and selected Chicago area secondary school students and students who will matriculate at the City Colleges of Chicago, particularly women and underrepresented minorities, develop strong math and problem solving skills while preparing for college. This camp works to ensure that our first-year students are on equal footing in mathematics when the fall 2010 semester begins. It encourages, supports, and assists the participants from selected Chicago area secondary schools and the City Colleges of Chicago to develop and hone their math skills so they will be successful if they decide to pursue a STEM related field in the future. In addition, it introduces these students to IIT and all it has to offer in hopes that they will decide to apply to IIT to continue their education here. For all the student participants, this program helps them focus on achieving college readiness in calculus, pre-calculus, study skills and math related computer skills, while also providing information on topics such as making the transition from high school to college. In addition, it helps these students become aware of the fact that mathematical competence is a prerequisite for success in many academic majors and many professional paths.
